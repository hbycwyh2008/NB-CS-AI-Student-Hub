# Course FAQ

**Computer Science and AI Pathway — Student Hub**

---

## General

**Q: Where do I find assignments?**  
A: In GitHub Classroom repositories your teacher creates — not in the private curriculum repo.

**Q: Where do I put my code?**  
A: In **your own** GitHub portfolio repository. See [Student_GitHub_Guide.md](Student_GitHub_Guide.md).

**Q: Can I copy code from the internet or AI?**  
A: You may use examples to **learn**, but you must **understand**, **test**, and **explain** your final work. Undisclosed copying is not acceptable.

---

## GitHub

**Q: What is the difference between the course repo and my repo?**  
A: Course materials are **read-only instructions**. Your repo is **your evidence** — code, commits, reflections.

**Q: How often should I commit?**  
A: At least once per class session, ideally after each meaningful change.

---

## AI Tools

**Q: Can I use ChatGPT or Cursor?**  
A: Yes — when it **helps you learn**. See [AI_Use_Policy_Student_Version.md](AI_Use_Policy_Student_Version.md).

**Q: Can AI write my whole assignment?**  
A: No. You must read, test, revise, and explain all submitted work.

---

## Assessment

**Q: Is it enough if my code runs?**  
A: No. You must show **mastery**: explain, rebuild, debug, modify, and apply.

**Q: Will I be asked to explain code orally?**  
A: Yes. Oral checks verify **your** understanding.

---

## Getting Stuck

**Q: What should I do when I get an error?**  
A: Read the message → isolate the problem → change one thing → ask for a hint (not a full fix).

**Q: When should I ask the teacher?**  
A: After you have tried debugging and documented what you attempted.

---

## Portfolio & Showcase

**Q: Do I need Notion?**  
A: Recommended for public showcase; ask your teacher for school-specific requirements.

**Q: What is the final project?**  
A: Typically an **AI School Assistant** — a web app that answers questions from school documents with sources. Your teacher will share the student-facing specification.
